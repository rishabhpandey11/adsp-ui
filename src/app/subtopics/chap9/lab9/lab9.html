Chapter 9 : Lab

Objective

Apply allpass filter theory and frequency warping techniques through hands-on Python exercises. You will design fractional delay filters, analyze group delay behavior, implement frequency warping, and apply Bark-scale approximations for audio processing.

Exercise 1: FIR Fractional Delay Filter

Goal: Implement a windowed sinc fractional delay filter and study the effect of filter length.

Task: Design an FIR fractional delay filter for a delay of 3.7 samples using a sine window.

Questions:

1. What happens at high frequencies? Is this a perfect allpass filter?

2. Try increasing L to 30. How does this affect the frequency response?

3. Can you visually verify the delay of 7.7 samples in the output?

Exercise 2: IIR Allpass Fractional Delay Filter

Goal: Implement the IIR fractional delay allpass filter and compare with FIR.

Task: Create the allp_delayfilt function and test it for various delay values.

Questions:

1. Is the magnitude truly constant at 1 (0 dB)? Compare with FIR from Exercise 1.

2. How does the impulse response differ from FIR?

3. Can you observe the fractional delay in the test signal output?

Exercise 3: Group Delay Analysis

Goal: Compute and visualize the group delay of IIR allpass filters.

Task: Analyze how group delay varies with frequency for different allpass coefficients.

Questions:

1.At what frequency range is the group delay constant?

2. How does group delay change at high frequencies?

3. Why does group delay increase toward Nyquist frequency?

Exercise 4: Frequency Warping - Bark Scale Visualization

Goal: Implement the warping phase function and visualize Bark-scale approximation.

Task: Plot the Bark scale and compare it with allpass warping curves.

Questions:

1. How does the Bark scale emphasize low frequencies?

2. Which allpass coefficient best approximates the Bark scale shape?

3. Verify the inverse property numerically. What is the maximum error?
