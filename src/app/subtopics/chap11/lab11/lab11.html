Chapter 11 : Lab

Objective

Apply Hilbert Transform concepts through hands-on Python exercises. You will implement Hilbert transformers, create analytic signals, extract instantaneous amplitude and phase, and explore applications in signal analysis and modulation.

Exercise 1: Understanding the Hilbert Transform Impulse Response

Goal: Construct and visualize the impulse response of a finite-length Hilbert transformer.

Task: Create a Hilbert transformer for n = -2...20 and analyze its properties.

Questions:

1. Why are only odd-indexed values non-zero?

2. What is the phase shift at different frequencies? Does it match ±90°?

3. Why is the magnitude response not flat across all frequencies?

Exercise 2: Creating an Analytic Signal

Goal: Construct an analytic signal manually by combining a real signal with its Hilbert transform.

Task: Create an analytic signal from a sinusoid and verify it has only positive frequencies.

Questions:

1. Is the imaginary part approximately a 90° phase-shifted version of the real part?

2. Does the analytic signal have energy only at positive frequencies?

3.Why might there be small differences between manual and scipy methods?

Exercise 3: Instantaneous Amplitude Extraction

Goal: Use the Hilbert transform to measure the instantaneous amplitude of a time-varying signal.

Task: Create an amplitude-modulated signal and extract its envelope using the analytic signal.

Questions:

1.How accurately does the extracted envelope match the true envelope?

2. Why is the instantaneous frequency approximately constant at the carrier frequency?

3. What happens if you increase the modulation frequency f_mod to 50 Hz?
