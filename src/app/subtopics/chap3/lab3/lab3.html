Chapter 3 : Lab

Objective

Apply SNR analysis and non-uniform quantization concepts through hands-on Python exercises. You will compute SNR for different signal types, implement μ-law companding, and compare uniform vs. non-uniform quantizers.

Exercise 1: SNR Penalty from Signal Backoff

Goal: Investigate how SNR degrades when signals don't use the full quantizer range.

Task: Test different signal amplitudes and verify the 6 dB per factor-of-2 rule.

Questions:

1. Verify the rule: SNR penalty = -20 log₁₀(backoff) dB

2. At what backoff factor does the signal become severely degraded?

3. Why is using full-scale range important for maximizing SNR?

Exercise 2: Companding vs. Uniform - SNR Comparison

Goal: Compare SNR performance across different signal amplitudes for both methods.

Task: Sweep signal amplitude and measure SNR for uniform vs. μ-law quantization.

Questions:

1. At what signal level is μ-law most beneficial?

2. Why does uniform quantization SNR degrade with amplitude backoff?

3. How does μ-law maintain more constant SNR across amplitudes?

Exercise 3: Monte Carlo Verification of SNR Theory

Goal: Use Monte Carlo simulation to verify theoretical SNR formulas.

Task: Run repeated trials with random signals and confirm the 6.02N rule empirically.

Questions:

1. Does the empirical slope match the theoretical 6.02 dB/bit?

2. Why is there small trial-to-trial variation in SNR?

3. How would the results change for a sinusoidal signal?
