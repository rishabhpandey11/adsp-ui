Chapter 10 : Lab

Objective

Explore minimum phase filter design, understand the relationship between pole-zero locations and filter properties, and implement minimum phase conversion algorithms. You will analyze group delay, create stable inverse filters, and compare minimum phase with linear phase filters.

Exercise 1: Linear Phase Filter Analysis

Goal: Create and analyze a linear phase filter to understand its zero distribution.

Task: Design a windowed sinc filter and examine its pole-zero plot and phase characteristics

Questions:

1. Is the impulse response symmetric? What does this tell you about the phase?

2. Identify the zero pair at approximately $r$ and $1/r$. What are their values?

3. Is the group delay constant across all frequencies? What does this mean?

Exercise 2: Using scipy.signal.minimum_phase

Goal:Use SciPy's built-in function to create minimum phase filters.

Task:Compare the manual method with SciPy's minimum_phase function.

Questions:

1. Why do the two methods produce slightly different results?

2.Why do the coefficients have opposite signs?

3. Which method is more accurate? Which is more convenient?

Exercise 3:Audio Equalization Application

Goal: Apply minimum phase filtering to audio equalization.

Task: Simulate a channel distortion and design a minimum phase equalizer to compensate.

Questions:

1.How does the equalizer compensate for channel distortion?

2. Why is minimum phase important for equalization?

3. What would happen if you used the inverse of a linear phase filter?
